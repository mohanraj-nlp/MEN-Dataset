KUALA LUMPUR: About 90 per cent of the country's teenagers aged between 12 and 17 have received their Covid-19 vaccines.  Health Minister Khairy Jamaluddin said the country has reached a milestone with 90 per cent of them having received at least one dose of the vaccine.  'For seniors, get your booster as soon as it is available,' he said via Twitter today.  To date, 86.5 per cent of the teenage population have completed their vaccination while 10 per cent are unvaccinated, according to the CovidNow portal updated yesterday.  As of Dec 5, 77.9 per cent of the country's total population had been fully vaccinated while 79.2 per cent have received at least one dose.  There are 25,443,839 fully vaccinated individuals, 436,050 partially vaccinated while 7,317,045 are unvaccinated.  According to latest projections, Malaysia will reach 80 per cent or 26,125,920 fully vaccinated individuals by May 24 next year - 169 more days to go.  A total 54,091,375 Covid-19 vaccine doses have been administered as of yesterday, with 2,958,239 being booster shots.
